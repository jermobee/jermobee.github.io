Fallout / Paint Overspray Removal

 

WHAT IS FALLOUT?

The term fallout refers to tiny airborne particles of steel, iron and other metal alloys. The most common source of these is from railway lines, which is why this phenomenon is often called 'rail dust'. The effect of the train's wheels on the rails, the train's brakes and the electrical pickups scraping on the overhead power lines produces tiny, often microscopic metal particles which carry a negative charge, while your car has a positive charge. This means that your car acts like a magnet, attracting all these particles to your paintwork.

Railways are not the only source of this dust. Just about any process where metal moves against metal will produce fallout. Even your own car's brakes and engine produces fallout, as will old trucks and buses that pump out black smoke.

WHY SHOULD FALLOUT BE TREATED?

Paintwork is not solid. If you look at it under a microscope you would see that it is full of holes like a sponge and as your paintwork corrodes, the oxidizing compounds seep deeper into these holes. Also, as the paintwork erodes under the metal particles, they bury themselves deeper into the paintwork. This makes it difficult to remove as it cannot simply be washed off. As the oxidization process requires water, washing you car will actually speed up the process.

THE CURE

A three stage removal process is used. First stage is an acid based chemical, second stage is an acid neutralizing soap and third stage is a soft compound.

PREVENTION

A paint sealant will act as protective barrier, but because paint sealants are similar in chemical composition to paintwork, these too will be eroded in time. Hopefully by this time it will have done it s job. Your car should be kept clean and the level of contamination should be checked upon at least once every few months. We recomend that a paint sealant be applied once a year.

Paint Over-Spray Removal

Many vehicles today are subject to paint over-spray from freshly painted bridges, buildings, and roadways. Left untouched the paint can imbed itself deep into clear coated paints making it almost impossible to remove. Our claying procedure will easily remove most paint over-spray quickly and safely. We recommend one of our exterior protection services after this procedure.
